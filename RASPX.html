RASP X

Custom-Fit Glacier Glasses for maximum protection and style in extreme conditions.

Glacier Sunglasses

Alpinism is a sport where the conditions are extreme. The main skin cancers come from UV exposure of the sun. In the sport of alpinism the parts of the body that suffer most from UV exposure are the cheeks and the nose. proper sunglasses for the sport must cover these.

Rasp X, showcases innovative features, including a fully integrated nose cover and side shields, custom-fit lens, and a sportive design. The sunglasses are made by 3D scanning the head of the user and modeling the sunglasses in Rhino 3D around the scanned face. With this method, the final product is a perfect custom fit design personalized to the customer.

Methodology

The methodology used for the project is specifically intended for redesigning a product. Usually, design methodologies start from the defining of an unsolved problem. In this case, the problem is already solved but the designer wants to go further and the intention is to solve it better or add extra functionalities to the design.

Design Challenge

The designer seeks to enhance the safety of alpinists by minimizing their exposure to harmful UV rays from the sun. This will be achieved by redesigning the glacier glasses into a more aesthetic and specifically useful product for the conditions required.

”The desire for a pair of glacier glasses with a nose cover that exudes style, allure, and fashionable design. Something entirely new and unseen.”

Ideation

3D sketching is possible thanks to SubD modeling in Rhino. This 3D modeling sketching strategy takes advantage of the design freedom that SubD provides. With the 2D sketches as reference, the same concepts are made in 3D. Every detail of the idea is shown better and can be modified with an immediate visual response.

Prototype

Because the sunglasses are custom fit to the face of the user, the frame and temples of the sunglasses are meant to be 3D printed with FDM or SLS additive manufacturing.

The materials chosen for the prototype and the mold of the lens is Polycarbonate although the final material for the frame is Nylon 12. The lens in the prototype has been made with a pressure former and a high infill 3D printed mold. The pressure adapts the clear PET (transparent material) into the mold creating a clear lens.

By bridging the gap between functionality and aesthetics, this design establishes a foundation for future innovations in sports eyewear and underscores the potential of 3D printing in industrial design.
